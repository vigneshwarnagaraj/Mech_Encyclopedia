Thermal · 11
Convection

Newton's cooling law, Nusselt correlations for forced and natural convection, and extended surface (fin) analysis.

Newton's law of cooling

\[ \dot{Q}=hA_s(T_s-T_\infty) \]
Convective heat transfer: \(h\) = convection coefficient [W/m²·K], \(A_s\) = surface area [m²], \(T_s\) = surface temperature [K], \(T_\infty\) = fluid free-stream temperature [K].
Typical \(h\) values:
  • Free convection in air: 5–25 W/m²·K
  • Forced convection in air: 25–250 W/m²·K
  • Forced convection in water: 500–20 000 W/m²·K
  • Boiling water: 2 500–100 000 W/m²·K

Nusselt number correlations

\[ Nu=\frac{hL}{k}\quad\Rightarrow\quad h=\frac{Nu\cdot k}{L} \]
Nusselt number: Dimensionless convection coefficient. Found from empirical correlations using Re and Pr, then used to extract \(h\). \(L\) = characteristic length [m], \(k\) = fluid thermal conductivity [W/m·K].

Forced convection — external flat plate

\[ Nu_L=0.664\,Re_L^{1/2}Pr^{1/3}\;\text{(laminar)},\quad Nu_L=0.037\,Re_L^{4/5}Pr^{1/3}\;\text{(turbulent)} \]
Flat plate correlations: Valid for Pr > 0.6. Laminar: \(Re_L < 5\times10^5\). Turbulent: \(5\times10^5 < Re_L < 10^7\). These give the average Nu over the plate length \(L\).
Uniform wall temperature · Pr > 0.6

Forced convection — internal pipe flow (Dittus-Boelter)

\[ Nu_D=0.023\,Re_D^{0.8}Pr^n,\quad n=0.4\text{ (heating)},\;n=0.3\text{ (cooling)} \]
Dittus-Boelter correlation: Valid for turbulent pipe flow (\(Re > 10\,000\)), \(0.7\leq Pr\leq 160\), \(L/D > 10\). \(D\) = pipe diameter [m]. For more accurate result with property variation, use the Gnielinski correlation.
\[ Nu_D=\frac{(f/8)(Re_D-1000)Pr}{1+12.7\sqrt{f/8}(Pr^{2/3}-1)}\quad\text{(Gnielinski)} \]
Gnielinski correlation: More accurate for \(3000 < Re < 5\times10^6\) and \(0.5 < Pr < 2000\). \(f\) = Darcy friction factor from Colebrook equation.

Natural convection

\[ Ra=Gr\cdot Pr=\frac{g\beta\Delta T L^3}{\nu\alpha},\quad \beta=\frac{1}{T}\;\text{(ideal gas)} \]
Rayleigh number: \(\beta\) = volumetric thermal expansion coefficient [K⁻¹]. \(\nu\) = kinematic viscosity [m²/s]. \(\alpha\) = thermal diffusivity [m²/s]. Combines buoyancy driving force with viscous and thermal resistance.
\[ Nu=C\,Ra^n \]
Natural convection correlations: Constants \(C\) and \(n\):
  • Vertical plate, laminar (\(10^4 < Ra < 10^9\)): \(C=0.59,\;n=0.25\)
  • Vertical plate, turbulent (\(Ra > 10^9\)): \(C=0.10,\;n=1/3\)
  • Horizontal plate (heated, facing up): \(C=0.54,\;n=0.25\) (laminar)

Extended surfaces — fins

\[ m=\sqrt{\frac{hP}{kA_c}},\quad \eta_{fin}=\frac{\tanh(mL_c)}{mL_c} \]
Fin efficiency: \(\eta_{fin}\) = fraction of fin operating at its maximum (base) temperature difference. \(P\) = fin perimeter [m], \(A_c\) = fin cross-section area [m²], \(L_c\) = corrected fin length (\(L + A_c/P\) for rectangular fin).
Longer, thinner fins have lower efficiency but larger area — there is an optimum design.
\[ \dot{Q}_{fin}=\eta_{fin}\,h\,A_{fin}\,(T_b-T_\infty) \]
Total fin heat transfer: \(A_{fin}\) = total fin surface area, \(T_b\) = fin base temperature. Overall surface efficiency for an array: \(\eta_o = 1-(N A_{fin}/A_{tot})(1-\eta_{fin})\).
