Newton-Raphson iteration (nonlinear solver)

\[ [K_T]\{\Delta u\}=\{R\}=\{F_{ext}\}-\{F_{int}\} \]
Incremental equilibrium: \([K_T]\) = tangent stiffness matrix (updated each iteration), \(\{R\}\) = residual force vector (out-of-balance force). Converges when \(|\{R\}|/|\{F_{ext}\}| < \text{tolerance}\). ANSYS default tolerance: 0.5% of reaction forces. Divergence occurs when the load increment is too large — bisect the load step.
\[ \{\sigma\}^{n+1}=\{\sigma\}^n+[D_{ep}]\{\Delta\varepsilon\} \]
Incremental stress update (plasticity): \([D_{ep}]\) = elastic-plastic constitutive matrix — differs from the elastic \([D]\) when the stress state is on the yield surface. ANSYS uses a return-mapping algorithm to project trial elastic stresses back onto the yield surface.

Common mistake: Using bonded contact everywhere simplifies the model but prevents capturing real load paths. If surfaces can physically separate or slide, use frictionless or frictional contact — even if it costs more computation.

Plasticity models

\[ \sigma_y(\bar{\varepsilon}_p)=\sigma_{y0}+H\bar{\varepsilon}_p\quad\text{(bilinear isotropic hardening)} \]
Isotropic hardening: \(\sigma_{y0}\) = initial yield stress, \(H\) = tangent modulus (slope of plastic region), \(\bar{\varepsilon}_p\) = accumulated plastic strain. The yield surface expands uniformly. Use for monotonic loading.
\[ \sigma_y = \sigma_{y0}\quad\text{(yield surface translates — kinematic hardening)} \]
Kinematic hardening: The yield surface translates in stress space rather than expanding. Models the Bauschinger effect (lower yield stress in compression after tensile yielding). Use for cyclic loading, fatigue, ratcheting.

Result interpretation — what to check

  • Reaction forces = applied loads (Newton's 3rd law — if not, model has an error)
  • Maximum deformation is physically plausible — compare to hand calculation
  • Von Mises stress distribution makes physical sense — highest stress at expected load-path locations
  • Stress singularities identified — peak stress at sharp corners grows with mesh refinement → not physical
  • Contact status verified — expected open/closed regions confirmed in contact results
  • Mesh sensitivity checked — refine at stress peaks and confirm they plateau
  • Deformation scale factor checked — ANSYS auto-scales by default; set to "True Scale" to check physical magnitudes
