Lift, Drag & Aerodynamic Forces

Lift and drag equations, drag breakdown, compressible flow basics, and aerodynamics for aircraft and automobiles.

Lift and drag forces

\[ L=\tfrac{1}{2}\rho V^2 S\,C_L,\quad D=\tfrac{1}{2}\rho V^2 S\,C_D \]
Lift and drag: \(\rho\) = air density [kg/m³], \(V\) = freestream velocity [m/s], \(S\) = reference area [m²] (wing planform area for aircraft, frontal area for cars), \(C_L\) = lift coefficient [-], \(C_D\) = drag coefficient [-]. Dynamic pressure: \(q_\infty=\tfrac{1}{2}\rho V^2\).
\[ C_{D,total}=C_{D,friction}+C_{D,pressure}+C_{D,induced}+C_{D,wave} \]
Total drag breakdown:
  • Friction drag: Skin friction from boundary layer — dominated by wetted area and surface finish.
  • Pressure (form) drag: Pressure difference between front and rear — dominated by body shape, separation.
  • Induced drag: Arises from lift generation — wing tip vortices. Increases with \(C_L^2\).
  • Wave drag: Compressibility effect at Ma > 0.8 — shock waves.
\[ C_{D,i}=\frac{C_L^2}{\pi\,e\,AR},\quad AR=\frac{b^2}{S} \]
Induced drag coefficient: \(e\) = Oswald span efficiency factor (0.8–0.95 for practical wings), \(AR\) = aspect ratio, \(b\) = wing span [m]. High aspect ratio → low induced drag. Gliders: AR ≈ 30. Fighter jets: AR ≈ 2–3.

Compressible flow basics

\[ a=\sqrt{\gamma RT},\quad Ma=\frac{V}{a},\quad \gamma_{air}=1.4,\quad R_{air}=287\;\text{J/kg·K} \]
Speed of sound: At 15 °C: \(a\approx340\) m/s. At 50 °C: \(a\approx361\) m/s. Compressibility effects become significant at Ma > 0.3 (density change > 5%).
\[ \frac{T_0}{T}=1+\frac{\gamma-1}{2}Ma^2,\quad \frac{p_0}{p}=\left(1+\frac{\gamma-1}{2}Ma^2\right)^{\gamma/(\gamma-1)} \]
Isentropic stagnation relations: \(T_0, p_0\) = total (stagnation) conditions. In high-speed CFD (Fluent with compressible ideal gas), these relations govern the thermodynamic state at boundaries. For Ma > 0.3, use mass flow inlet BC specified with total pressure and temperature.
Isentropic · ideal gas · no heat transfer or friction
\[ \frac{A}{A^*}=\frac{1}{Ma}\left[\frac{2}{\gamma+1}\left(1+\frac{\gamma-1}{2}Ma^2\right)\right]^{(\gamma+1)/[2(\gamma-1)]} \]
Area-Mach number relation (isentropic nozzle): \(A^*\) = throat area (Ma=1). For a converging-diverging nozzle, the throat must be choked (Ma=1) before supersonic flow develops in the diverging section.
