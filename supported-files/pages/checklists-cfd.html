CFD Setup & Validation Checklists

Use before running, after running, and before reporting any CFD result.

Pre-Run CFD Setup Checklist

  • Geometry clean: No sliver faces, gaps, overlapping volumes, or unnecessarily small features that will cause mesh quality issues.
  • Fluid domain correct: Enclosure created (external flow) or internal volume extracted (internal flow); Boolean operations verified; no solid material inside fluid domain.
  • Named selections assigned: All inlets, outlets, walls, symmetry planes, and rotating domains named before meshing — not after.
  • Inflation layers present: Prism layers on all viscous walls; first-layer height calculated for target y+ using friction velocity estimate.
  • Mesh quality verified: Minimum orthogonal quality > 0.15; skewness < 0.85; no negative volume cells.
  • Growth rate acceptable: ≤ 1.2 in regions of interest; ≤ 1.3 globally.
  • Turbulence model chosen intentionally: SST k-ω for separated/adverse pressure gradient flows; k-ε Realizable for enclosed turbulent ducts; Laminar for Re < critical.
  • Boundary conditions physically correct: Inlet BC type matches known quantity (velocity / mass flow / total pressure); outlet at 0 gauge static pressure for most cases.
  • Fluid properties entered: Density, viscosity, thermal conductivity, Cp — constant at operating condition or temperature-dependent if ΔT > 50 °C.
  • Energy equation status correct: Enabled if temperature gradients, heat flux, buoyancy, or wall heating exists. Off for isothermal incompressible flows.
  • Gravity enabled if needed: Natural convection, free surface, buoyant plumes — define direction vector correctly (−y for vertical down).
  • Convergence monitors set: At least one physical monitor (ΔP, drag, T_out) in addition to residuals. Monitors saved to file.
  • Iteration count sufficient: Minimum 500 iterations for simple steady; 1000–3000 for complex geometry or separated flow.
  • Interpolation scheme: First Order Upwind for initial convergence; switch to Second Order Upwind for final result.
  • Mesh independence study planned: Three mesh variants (coarse/medium/fine) identified; target output quantity chosen before running.

Post-Run CFD Validation Checklist

  • Residuals dropped ≥ 3 orders of magnitude AND physical monitors have fully stabilised.
  • Mass conservation: Inlet mass flow = outlet mass flow ± 0.1% (use Flux Reports in Fluent).
  • Energy conservation (if thermal): Net heat in = net heat out ± 2%.
  • Velocity profiles physically reasonable: Parabolic in laminar pipe; fuller log-law profile in turbulent boundary layer.
  • Pressure distribution makes physical sense: Stagnation at leading edge; low pressure on suction surface; recovery in diffuser.
  • No negative absolute pressure cells: Indicates cavitation risk or a boundary condition error.
  • y+ in correct range for model: SST k-ω: y⁺ ≈ 1–5; k-ε wall functions: y⁺ = 30–300. Check using Contours → y+ on wall surfaces.
  • Symmetry confirmed if used: Mirror-image results on both sides of symmetry plane.
  • Mesh independence confirmed: < 2–5% change in target output between medium and fine mesh.
  • Result compared to theory or reference: Darcy-Weisbach for pipe flow; Blasius for flat plate BL thickness; published drag data for known geometry.

CFD Reporting Checklist

  • State turbulence model and justify the choice explicitly.
  • State mesh cell count, y+ distribution (min/max/average), and growth rate.
  • Show mesh independence plot: 3 mesh levels, target output vs. cell count.
  • Show residual convergence plot and physical monitor convergence plot.
  • Report integrated quantities (drag, ΔP, heat transfer rate) — not just contour images.
  • Compare at least one result to analytical solution, published experiment, or known correlation.
  • Identify limitations: geometry simplifications, turbulence model limitations, 2D vs 3D assumptions.
  • State operating conditions: fluid properties, inlet conditions, Re, Ma if relevant.
