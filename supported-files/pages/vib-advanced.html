Logarithmic decrement — damping from free decay

\[ \delta=\ln\!\left(\frac{x_1}{x_2}\right)=\frac{2\pi\zeta}{\sqrt{1-\zeta^2}}\approx2\pi\zeta\;\text{(light damping)} \]
Log decrement \(\delta\): Natural log of ratio of successive peak amplitudes in a free decay signal. Gives \(\zeta\) from experimental data without knowing \(m\), \(k\), or \(c\) individually. Approximate formula \(\delta\approx2\pi\zeta\) is valid for \(\zeta < 0.1\).
\[ \delta=\frac{1}{n}\ln\!\left(\frac{x_1}{x_{n+1}}\right),\quad\zeta=\frac{\delta}{\sqrt{4\pi^2+\delta^2}} \]
Multi-cycle averaging (exact): \(n\) = number of cycles between measured peaks. More accurate than single-cycle method — averages out measurement noise. Use at least 5 cycles for \(\zeta < 0.02\); fewer cycles for heavily damped systems.

Base excitation

\[ m\ddot{z}+c\dot{z}+kz=-m\ddot{y},\quad z=x-y \]
Base excitation model (relative motion): \(y(t)\) = base displacement, \(x(t)\) = mass absolute displacement, \(z=x-y\) = relative (rattle space) displacement. The equation has the same form as forced vibration with \(-m\ddot{y}\) as the forcing. Used for vehicle suspension, seismic analysis, and equipment on vibrating platforms.
\[ \frac{X}{Y}=TR=\sqrt{\frac{1+(2\zeta r)^2}{(1-r^2)^2+(2\zeta r)^2}} \]
Displacement transmissibility: Ratio of absolute mass amplitude to base amplitude. For isolation (\(r > \sqrt{2}\)): less than 1. For \(r \gg \sqrt{2}\): \(TR \approx 1/r^2\) — mass moves very little compared to the vibrating base.

Random vibration and Power Spectral Density

\[ S_{xx}(f)=\lim_{T\to\infty}\frac{|X_T(f)|^2}{T},\quad\sigma_x^2=\int_0^\infty S_{xx}(f)\,df \]
Power Spectral Density (PSD): \(S_{xx}(f)\) [m²/Hz or g²/Hz]. Describes how mean-square response distributes across frequency. Total mean-square response = area under the PSD curve. \(\sigma_x\) = RMS value [m or g] = standard deviation for zero-mean signal.
\[ S_{yy}(f)=|H(f)|^2\,S_{xx}(f) \]
Response PSD from input PSD: The output PSD = magnitude squared of the FRF × input PSD. Peak in \(S_{yy}\) at resonance is amplified by \(1/(2\zeta)^2\) relative to away-from-resonance. This is the basis of ANSYS Random Vibration analysis: input a base excitation PSD, ANSYS computes the response PSD and RMS stress/displacement.
\[ \sigma^2_{response}=\int_0^\infty S_{yy}(f)\,df\approx\frac{\pi}{2}\,S_0\,\omega_n\,Q \]
Miles' equation (white noise approximation): \(S_0\) = PSD level at resonance [g²/Hz], \(Q=1/(2\zeta)\) = quality factor. Gives a quick RMS response estimate for a lightly damped SDOF system excited by flat-spectrum random input. Used extensively in aerospace qualification testing.

Rotordynamics basics

\[ \omega_{cr}=\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}},\quad r=\frac{\omega}{\omega_{cr}} \]
Critical speed: Shaft speed at which the excitation frequency (1× running speed) coincides with a bending natural frequency. At \(r=1\): resonance — large lateral deflections (whirl). Subcritical operation: \(r < 0.7\); supercritical: \(r > 1.4\) (must pass through resonance to reach operating speed).
\[ e_{residual}=\frac{U}{m}\quad[\text{m}],\quad U=m_u\,e \]
Residual imbalance: \(U\) = imbalance [kg·m], \(e\) = eccentricity [m], \(m_u\) = imbalance mass [kg]. ISO 1940 specifies balance quality grades: G6.3 for general machinery, G2.5 for electric motors, G0.4 for precision spindles. Balancing quality grade: \(G = e\,\omega_{max}/1000\) [mm/s].
