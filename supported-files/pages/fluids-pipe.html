Fluid Mechanics · 06
Pipe Flow & Head Losses

Internal flow, friction factors, velocity profiles, minor losses, and pipe network equations.

Friction factor and major losses

\[ h_f=f\frac{L}{D}\frac{V^2}{2g},\quad \Delta p_f = f\frac{L}{D}\frac{\rho V^2}{2} \]
Darcy-Weisbach equation: \(h_f\) = head loss due to friction [m], \(f\) = Darcy friction factor [-], \(L\) = pipe length [m], \(D\) = pipe diameter [m], \(V\) = mean velocity [m/s]. Most general pipe friction formula — works for laminar and turbulent.
Laminar and turbulentm (head) or Pa (pressure)
\[ f_{laminar}=\frac{64}{Re} \]
Laminar friction factor: Valid for \(Re < 2300\). Exact analytical result derived from the Hagen-Poiseuille parabolic velocity profile. No roughness dependence in laminar flow.
\[ \frac{1}{\sqrt{f}}=-2\log\!\left(\frac{\varepsilon/D}{3.7}+\frac{2.51}{Re\sqrt{f}}\right) \]
Colebrook-White equation: Implicit formula for turbulent friction factor. \(\varepsilon\) = absolute roughness [m] (steel pipe: \(\varepsilon \approx 0.046\) mm, drawn tubing: \(\varepsilon \approx 0.0015\) mm). Solved iteratively. The Moody chart is the graphical solution.
Turbulent, full-pipe flow, circular cross-section
\[ f_{Blasius}\approx\frac{0.316}{Re^{0.25}}\quad (Re<10^5,\text{ smooth pipe}) \]
Blasius explicit approximation: Good to ±5% for smooth pipes and Re < 10⁵. Useful for quick estimates without iteration.

Velocity profiles in pipes

\[ u(r)=u_{max}\!\left(1-\frac{r^2}{R^2}\right),\quad u_{max}=2V_{avg} \]
Hagen-Poiseuille profile (laminar): Parabolic. Maximum velocity at centreline = 2× average. \(R\) = pipe radius [m], \(r\) = radial position. Driven by pressure gradient: \(\Delta p / L = 128\mu Q/(\pi D^4)\).
Laminar · fully developed · circular pipe
\[ \frac{u}{u_\tau}=\frac{u_\tau y}{\nu}\;\text{(viscous sublayer, }y^+<5\text{)},\quad \frac{u}{u_\tau}=2.44\ln(y^+)+5.0\;\text{(log law, }y^+>30\text{)} \]
Law of the wall (turbulent): The velocity profile in a turbulent boundary layer is divided into: viscous sublayer (\(y^+<5\)), buffer layer (5–30), and log-law region (\(y^+>30\)). This profile structure is what CFD near-wall models are built on.

Minor (local) losses

\[ h_m=K_L\frac{V^2}{2g},\quad \Delta p_m = K_L\frac{\rho V^2}{2} \]
Minor head loss: \(K_L\) = loss coefficient for fittings, bends, valves, expansions, contractions.
Typical values: sharp inlet entry \(\approx0.5\), re-entrant inlet \(\approx0.9\), 90° standard elbow \(\approx0.9\), gate valve (fully open) \(\approx0.2\), globe valve \(\approx10\), sudden expansion: \(K_L=(1-A_1/A_2)^2\).
\[ h_L=\sum h_f + \sum h_m = \sum f_i\frac{L_i}{D_i}\frac{V_i^2}{2g}+\sum K_{L,j}\frac{V_j^2}{2g} \]
Total head loss in a pipe system: Sum of all major (friction) and minor losses. Used in pump selection: pump head required \(H_{pump}=h_L+\Delta z+(p_2-p_1)/(\rho g)\).
