Fluid Mechanics · 04
Fluid Properties & Pressure

Fundamental fluid properties, pressure relations, hydrostatics, and the ideal gas law.

Fluid properties

\[ \rho=\frac{m}{V},\quad \gamma=\rho g,\quad \nu=\frac{\mu}{\rho},\quad SG=\frac{\rho}{\rho_{water}} \]
Symbols: \(\rho\) = density [kg/m³], \(\gamma\) = specific weight [N/m³], \(\mu\) = dynamic viscosity [Pa·s], \(\nu\) = kinematic viscosity [m²/s], \(SG\) = specific gravity (dimensionless).
Reference values: \(\rho_{water,20°C} \approx 998\) kg/m³ · \(\rho_{air,15°C} \approx 1.225\) kg/m³.
\[ \tau = \mu\frac{du}{dy} \]
Newton's law of viscosity: \(\tau\) = shear stress [Pa], \(du/dy\) = velocity gradient normal to flow direction [s⁻¹]. This defines a Newtonian fluid — viscosity is constant. Non-Newtonian fluids (blood, polymer melts, ketchup) have viscosity that depends on shear rate.
Newtonian fluidPa
\[ \beta = -\frac{1}{V}\frac{dV}{dp}\bigg|_T \quad\text{(bulk modulus: } K = 1/\beta\text{)} \]
Compressibility: \(\beta\) = compressibility [Pa⁻¹], \(K\) = bulk modulus [Pa]. For water: \(K \approx 2.2\) GPa (nearly incompressible). For air at 1 atm: \(K = \gamma p \approx 142\) kPa (easily compressed). In CFD: incompressible assumption is valid when Ma < 0.3.

Pressure — definitions and hydrostatics

\[ p_{abs} = p_{gauge} + p_{atm},\quad p_{atm} = 101\,325\;\text{Pa} \]
Absolute vs gauge pressure: CFD solvers (Fluent) work with gauge pressure (zero = atmospheric). Always clarify which is reported when comparing to measurements or hand calculations.
\[ p=p_0+\rho g h \]
Hydrostatic pressure: \(p_0\) = surface pressure [Pa], \(h\) = depth below free surface [m]. Pressure increases linearly with depth. Valid for static fluid only — any flow requires the full Bernoulli or Navier-Stokes equation.
Static fluid onlyPa
\[ F_{hydro}=\rho g \bar{h}\, A,\quad y_{cp}-\bar{y}=\frac{I_{xx,c}}{\bar{y}A} \]
Force on submerged plane surface: \(\bar{h}\) = depth of centroid [m], \(A\) = area [m²]. Force acts at the centre of pressure \(y_{cp}\), which is below the centroid. \(I_{xx,c}\) = second moment of area about centroidal axis.
\[ F_B = \rho g V_{submerged} \]
Archimedes / buoyancy force: \(F_B\) [N]. Acts upward through the centre of buoyancy (centroid of displaced volume). In CFD with gravity enabled, buoyancy is applied through the body force term in N-S.

Ideal gas law

\[ p = \rho R T,\quad R_{air}=287\;\text{J/kg·K},\quad pV=nR_uT \]
Ideal gas law per unit mass: \(T\) = absolute temperature [K], \(R\) = specific gas constant [J/kg·K], \(R_u = 8.314\) J/mol·K = universal gas constant.
When it fails: High pressure (dense gases), near condensation, or for real-gas effects in cryogenic or supercritical flows.
Ideal gas — no intermolecular forces
\[ c_p - c_v = R,\quad \gamma=\frac{c_p}{c_v},\quad c_p=\frac{\gamma R}{\gamma-1} \]
Specific heat relations for ideal gas: For air: \(\gamma=1.4\), \(c_p=1005\) J/kg·K, \(c_v=718\) J/kg·K. Used in compressible flow and thermodynamic cycle analysis.

Surface tension and capillarity

\[ \Delta p = \frac{2\sigma}{r}\;\text{(bubble)},\quad \Delta p = \frac{4\sigma}{r}\;\text{(soap film)},\quad h=\frac{4\sigma\cos\theta}{\rho g d} \]
Surface tension \(\sigma\) [N/m]: \(r\) = radius [m], \(h\) = capillary rise [m], \(\theta\) = contact angle [°], \(d\) = tube diameter [m]. Relevant in droplet dynamics, inkjet printing, microfluidics, and multiphase CFD (VOF model).
