Thermal Basics

Temperature, heat, specific heat, thermodynamic laws, and thermal diffusivity — the foundation before conduction, convection, and radiation.

Fundamental thermal quantities

\[ Q=mc_p\Delta T \]
Sensible heat: \(Q\) = heat energy [J], \(m\) = mass [kg], \(c_p\) = specific heat at constant pressure [J/kg·K], \(\Delta T\) = temperature change [K or °C].
Reference values: Air \(c_p \approx 1005\) J/kg·K · Water \(c_p \approx 4182\) J/kg·K · Steel \(c \approx 500\) J/kg·K · Aluminum \(c \approx 900\) J/kg·K.
\[ Q=mL \]
Latent heat: Energy for phase change at constant temperature. \(L\) = latent heat [J/kg]. For water: \(L_{vap}\approx 2.26\times10^6\) J/kg (evaporation at 100 °C), \(L_{fus}\approx 3.34\times10^5\) J/kg (melting at 0 °C).
\[ \dot{Q}=\frac{dQ}{dt}\quad[\text{W}] \]
Heat transfer rate: \(\dot{Q}\) [W = J/s]. Power form used in steady-state thermal analysis. In ANSYS, thermal loads are specified as heat flux [W/m²] or heat generation rate [W/m³] or total heat [W] on a surface.

Temperature scales and conversions

\[ T[\text{K}]=T[°\text{C}]+273.15,\quad T[°\text{C}]=\tfrac{5}{9}(T[°\text{F}]-32) \]
Always use Kelvin [K] in radiation equations (\(T^4\)) and ideal gas law. Temperature differences \(\Delta T\) are numerically identical in K and °C. In ANSYS, specify whether input temperatures are in K or °C in the unit system settings.

Laws of thermodynamics (engineering application)

\[ \Delta U = Q - W \quad\text{(first law)} \]
First law: \(\Delta U\) = change in internal energy, \(Q\) = heat added to system, \(W\) = work done by system. Energy is conserved. In steady-flow devices: \(\dot{Q}-\dot{W}=\dot{m}[(h_2-h_1)+\tfrac{1}{2}(V_2^2-V_1^2)+g(z_2-z_1)]\).
\[ dS\geq\frac{dQ}{T}\;\text{(second law)},\quad \eta_{Carnot}=1-\frac{T_L}{T_H} \]
Second law: Entropy of an isolated system never decreases. The Carnot efficiency sets the maximum possible efficiency for any heat engine operating between \(T_H\) and \(T_L\) (both in Kelvin).

Thermal diffusivity

\[ \alpha=\frac{k}{\rho c_p}\quad[\text{m}^2/\text{s}] \]
Thermal diffusivity: Ratio of heat conducted through a material to heat stored per unit volume. High \(\alpha\) = fast thermal response (copper: \(\alpha\approx1.17\times10^{-4}\) m²/s). Low \(\alpha\) = slow response (brick: \(\alpha\approx5\times10^{-7}\) m²/s). Used in transient conduction Fourier number: \(Fo=\alpha t/L^2\).
