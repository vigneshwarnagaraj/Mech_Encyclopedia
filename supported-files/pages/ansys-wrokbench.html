Engineering Data — material input checklist

  • Density ρ: Required for any inertia, gravitational, or dynamic analysis. In kg/m³ for SI.
  • Young's modulus E and Poisson's ratio ν: Required for all structural analyses. Enter E in Pa (not GPa): 200 GPa = 2×10¹¹ Pa.
  • Thermal conductivity k and specific heat c_p: Required for thermal analyses. Check temperature dependence if operating above 200 °C.
  • Coefficient of thermal expansion α_th: Required for thermal-structural coupled analyses.
  • Yield strength σ_y: Required for failure assessment (FOS) and plasticity models.
  • S-N curve data: Required for fatigue analysis in Fatigue Tool.
  • Damping: Specified in Analysis Settings (constant damping ratio or Rayleigh α/β), not in Engineering Data.

Geometry preparation — SpaceClaim workflow

  1. Import CAD: File → Open. Supported formats: STEP, IGES, Parasolid, ACIS, SolidWorks, CATIA, NX. Choose "Import" over "Attach" to avoid link dependencies.
  2. Defeaturing: Remove small holes, fillets, and chamfers irrelevant to the analysis. Use Repair → Find Faults to identify geometry issues automatically.
  3. Share topology: For bodies that share a face, use Share to create conformal mesh interfaces — avoids contact pairs at interior faces.
  4. Fluid enclosure (CFD): Design → Enclosure → create external flow domain. Use Fill for internal flow domain extraction. Subtract solid bodies from the enclosure.
  5. Named selections: Create named selections in SpaceClaim — they transfer to Mechanical and Fluent automatically. Name inlets, outlets, walls, symmetry planes.

Named selections — critical workflow habit

Named selections are the single most important organisational habit in ANSYS. Create them for:

Structural

Fixed supports, frictionless supports, force application surfaces, pressure faces, symmetry planes, contact target and contact bodies, result probes.

CFD / Thermal

Inlets, outlets, walls, symmetry planes, periodic boundaries, heat source faces, monitor surfaces, rotating domains.

Why it matters: Named selections make BCs easy to verify, easy to modify, and easy for someone else to understand. A model without named selections requires reverse-engineering to identify what was applied where.
