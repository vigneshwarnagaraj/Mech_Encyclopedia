Engineering Glossary — CFD, FEA & Vibrations

Key terms defined precisely — the kind of definitions useful at work and in interviews.

CFD Glossary

y+
Dimensionless wall distance: y⁺ = u_τy/ν. Controls which region of the boundary layer the first cell occupies. y⁺ ≈ 1: viscous sublayer (wall-resolved); y⁺ = 30–300: log-law region (wall function range).
RANS
Reynolds-Averaged Navier-Stokes. Solves for the time-mean flow field; models turbulent fluctuations through an eddy viscosity closure. All standard industrial turbulence models (k-ε, k-ω, SST) are RANS-based.
LES
Large Eddy Simulation. Resolves turbulent eddies larger than the filter width (≈ mesh size); models subgrid scales. More accurate than RANS for separated, rotating, combusting, or acoustically significant flows. Much higher computational cost.
SST k-ω
Shear Stress Transport k-omega model (Menter, 1994). Uses k-ω near walls for accurate sublayer treatment; blends to k-ε in the freestream for robustness. Best general-purpose RANS model for external aerodynamics and separated flows.
Residual
Measure of imbalance in the discretised governing equations summed over all cells. Dropping 3–4 orders of magnitude is a necessary but not sufficient convergence indicator — physical monitors must also stabilise.
Inflation layer
Thin prismatic cells stacked normal to wall surfaces. Required to resolve the sharp velocity (and temperature) gradient in the boundary layer and to achieve the target y+ value.
GCI
Grid Convergence Index. Richardson extrapolation-based estimate of discretisation error between two mesh refinement levels. GCI < 5% is typically acceptable. Quantifies how much the result would change on an infinitely fine mesh.
SIMPLE
Semi-Implicit Method for Pressure-Linked Equations. Segregated pressure-velocity coupling algorithm. Sequentially updates pressure correction and velocity — most common algorithm for steady incompressible flow in Fluent.
CFL
Courant-Friedrichs-Lewy number: V·Δt/Δx. For explicit time-stepping: must be ≤ 1 for stability. Physically: how many cells does the flow travel per time step. Implicit methods do not have this constraint but accuracy degrades at large CFL.
Turbulence intensity
I = u'/U [%]. Ratio of RMS velocity fluctuation to mean velocity. Typical values: clean freestream 0.1–1%; pipe flow 3–5%; after bends or fittings 5–15%. Required as inlet BC for turbulence models alongside hydraulic diameter or length scale.
Wall function
Semi-empirical formula that bridges the viscous sublayer region to the log-law region without resolving it. Requires y⁺ = 30–300. Used with standard k-ε model. Avoids very fine near-wall mesh but introduces modelling assumptions.
Boussinesq hypothesis
Models Reynolds stresses as proportional to the mean strain rate via eddy viscosity μ_t. Fundamental assumption of all two-equation RANS models. Breaks down for flows with strong streamline curvature, rotation, or rapid change in strain rate.

FEA Glossary

DOF
Degree of Freedom. Number of independent displacement or rotation components at a node. 3D solid node: 3 translational DOFs. Shell node: 5–6 DOFs (3 translation + 2–3 rotation). Total model DOFs = nodes × DOF/node.
Singularity
A location where stress increases without bound as mesh is refined. Caused by sharp re-entrant corners, point loads, or point constraints. Not a physical stress — add a fillet radius or distribute the load over an area to resolve it.
Stiffness matrix
Global [K] assembled from element matrices. Represents the coupled spring network connecting all nodes. [K]{u} = {F} solves for all displacements given all loads. Singular if rigid body motion is possible (under-constrained).
Large Deflection
Geometric nonlinearity flag in ANSYS Mechanical. Enables strain calculation on the deformed geometry rather than the original configuration. Required when δ/L > 5%, for snap-through, buckling, flexible structures, or large rotation.
Contact status
Whether a contact pair is Open (no load transferred) or Closed (load transferred through contact pressure). Status can change during loading — this is contact nonlinearity. Requires Newton-Raphson iteration to resolve.
TET10
10-node quadratic tetrahedral element (ANSYS SOLID187). Has midside nodes — accurately models curved boundaries and bending. Default solid element in ANSYS Mechanical for imported CAD geometry. Preferred over linear TET4.
Von Mises stress
Equivalent stress σ_v = √[((σ₁−σ₂)²+(σ₂−σ₃)²+(σ₃−σ₁)²)/2]. Scalar failure indicator for ductile metals. Failure when σ_v ≥ σ_yield. Same as "Equivalent Stress" in ANSYS results.
FOS
Factor of Safety = σ_yield / σ_vonMises. Dimensionless margin. FOS = 1: at yield limit; FOS = 2: half yield stress used; FOS < 1: yielding predicted. Report with location and loading case.
Shear locking
Numerical pathology in linear (first-order) solid elements under bending. The element overpredicts shear strain and is artificially stiff. Resolved by using higher-order elements (TET10, HEXA20) or reduced integration.

Vibrations Glossary

ωₙ
Undamped natural frequency [rad/s]. Frequency at which a system vibrates freely without external forcing. Depends only on mass and stiffness: ωₙ = √(k/m). Does not depend on damping or forcing amplitude.
ζ (zeta)
Damping ratio = c/c_c. Dimensionless. ζ < 1: underdamped (oscillatory decay); ζ = 1: critically damped (fastest return without oscillation); ζ > 1: overdamped. Most engineering structures: ζ = 0.01–0.05.
FRF
Frequency Response Function H(ω) = X(ω)/F(ω). Complex ratio of response to input in the frequency domain. Magnitude peaks at natural frequencies; phase shifts 180° through each resonance. Measured experimentally to extract modal parameters.
Mode shape
Eigenvector {φ} of the eigenvalue problem [K−ω²M]{φ}=0. Describes the relative deformation pattern at a natural frequency. Normalised — only the shape matters, not the absolute magnitude. Orthogonal with respect to mass and stiffness matrices.
EMA
Experimental Modal Analysis. Extraction of natural frequencies, damping ratios, and mode shapes from measured FRFs using curve-fitting algorithms (peak-picking, circle fit, LSCF/PolyMAX). Used to validate and update FEA models.
PSD
Power Spectral Density [m²/Hz or g²/Hz]. Describes how mean-square response is distributed across frequency. Area under PSD curve = mean-square (variance). Used for random excitation specification in aerospace, automotive, and seismic analysis.
Log decrement
δ = ln(x₁/x₂) or (1/n)ln(x₁/x_{n+1}). Practical method to estimate damping ratio from a measured free-decay signal: ζ ≈ δ/(2π) for light damping. Does not require knowledge of mass, stiffness, or damping coefficient.
Transmissibility
TR = |output/input|. Ratio of transmitted force or motion to applied. TR < 1 at r > √2 — isolation region. TR = 1 at r = √2 for all ζ — crossover. Higher damping reduces resonance peak but degrades isolation at high frequency ratio.
MAC
Modal Assurance Criterion. Scalar (0–1) comparing two mode shape vectors. MAC = 1: identical shapes; MAC = 0: orthogonal. MAC > 0.9 indicates good FEA-to-test correlation for that mode. Low MAC indicates model or measurement error.
