Modal Analysis

Frequency response functions, modal participation factors, experimental modal analysis, and interpreting ANSYS modal results.

Frequency response function (FRF)

\[ H_{jk}(\omega)=\frac{X_j(\omega)}{F_k(\omega)}=\sum_{r=1}^n\frac{\phi_{jr}\phi_{kr}/M_r}{\omega_r^2-\omega^2+j2\zeta_r\omega_r\omega} \]
FRF as sum of modal contributions: Response at DOF \(j\) per unit force at DOF \(k\). Each mode contributes a resonant peak. At resonance \(\omega=\omega_r\): the \(r\)th mode dominates, with amplitude scaled by \(\phi_{jr}\phi_{kr}/(2\zeta_r\omega_r^2 M_r)\). Phase shifts by 180° through each resonance.
\[ H(\omega)=\frac{1}{k-\omega^2 m+j\omega c}\quad\text{(SDOF)} \]
SDOF receptance: Complex-valued — real part (co-quad) and imaginary part (quad). Magnitude: \(|H|=1/\sqrt{(k-\omega^2m)^2+(\omega c)^2}\). The imaginary part peaks at the natural frequency for lightly damped systems.

In practice: Accelerometers measure acceleration — so experimental FRFs are often accelerance. ANSYS Harmonic Response outputs displacement by default. Convert: velocity = displacement × \(j\omega\), acceleration = displacement × \((j\omega)^2=-\omega^2\).

Modal participation factor

\[ \Gamma_r=\frac{\{\phi_r\}^T[M]\{D\}}{M_r},\quad L_r=\{\phi_r\}^T[M]\{D\} \]
Modal participation factor \(\Gamma_r\): \(\{D\}\) = direction vector (e.g., \(\{1,1,1,\ldots\}\) for uniform base excitation in one axis). Indicates how strongly mode \(r\) is excited by the loading pattern. Effective mass: \(m_{eff,r} = L_r^2/M_r\). Sum of all effective masses = total system mass.
ANSYS modal output: After running a Modal analysis, check the "Participation Factor Summary" in Solution Information. Sort by effective mass fraction in the loading direction. Modes with low effective mass contribute little to that direction's response but may matter for structural coupling or cross-axis response.

Experimental modal analysis (EMA)

\[ \omega_r=\omega\bigg|_{\text{peak of }|H|}\approx\omega_{r,d},\quad \zeta_r=\frac{\Delta\omega}{2\omega_r}\;\text{(half-power method)} \]
Half-power bandwidth method: \(\Delta\omega\) = frequency bandwidth between the two frequencies where \(|H|=|H_{max}|/\sqrt{2}\) (i.e., 3 dB below peak). Provides a quick estimate of damping from a measured FRF. More accurate: circle-fit (Nyquist plot) or LSCF (PolyMAX).
\[ MAC(r,s)=\frac{|\{\phi_r\}^T\{\phi_s^{exp}\}|^2}{(\{\phi_r\}^T\{\phi_r\})(\{\phi_s^{exp}\}^T\{\phi_s^{exp}\})} \]
Modal Assurance Criterion (MAC): Compares analytical (FEA) and experimental mode shapes. MAC = 1: identical shapes. MAC = 0: orthogonal (completely different). MAC > 0.9 typically indicates good correlation. Used in FEA model validation and updating.
