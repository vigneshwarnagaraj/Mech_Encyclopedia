FEA Setup & Validation Checklists

Static structural, modal, harmonic response, and transient analysis checklists.

Static Structural Checklist

  • Material properties complete: E, ν, ρ for every body. Yield strength if checking failure. CTE if thermal loading.
  • Contact types verified: Bonded only where surfaces are truly joined; frictional/frictionless where separation or sliding is physically possible.
  • Mesh adequate at stress concentrations: Refine at fillets, holes, and notches. Minimum 3–5 elements through the thickness in bending regions.
  • Mesh quality checked: Element quality, skewness, Jacobian ratio in Mesh Metrics panel.
  • Boundary conditions physically correct: No rigid body motion possible (under-constrained); no over-constraint that artificially stiffens the model.
  • Loads applied correctly: Pressure [Pa] not force — check total resultant force matches expectation. Force direction and magnitude verified.
  • Large Deflection setting: ON if δ/L > 5% or if buckling, snap-through, or large rotation is possible.
  • Substeps sufficient for nonlinear: Initial substeps 10–20; allow bisection; maximum substeps 100–200 for strongly nonlinear problems.
  • Results objects inserted before solving: Total deformation, equivalent stress, principal stresses, reaction forces.
  • Reaction forces checked: Sum must equal applied loads (Newton's 3rd law). Any discrepancy indicates a model error.
  • Stress singularities identified: Sharp corners with increasing stress under mesh refinement → singularity, not physical stress.

Modal Analysis Checklist

  • Constraints physically correct: Free-free for suspended structure; fixed-base for grounded structure. Constraints fundamentally change mode shapes.
  • Sufficient modes extracted: Enough to cover the frequency range of interest plus 2–3 extra. Check effective mass fraction > 80–90% in loading directions.
  • Mode shapes visually inspected: First mode: largest motion, fewest nodes. Higher modes: more node lines. Shapes should respect geometry symmetry.
  • Frequency matches order-of-magnitude estimate: Use \(f_n \approx \sqrt{k/m}/(2\pi)\) with estimated stiffness and mass as a sanity check.
  • Pre-stress modal if applicable: Link Static Structural for centrifugal stiffening, tension-stiffened cables, or pressurised vessels.
  • Participation factor summary reviewed: Identify dominant modes in each excitation direction.

Harmonic Response Checklist

  • Frequency range covers all resonances of interest (confirmed from modal result).
  • Frequency resolution adequate near resonances: Use cluster points or fine frequency steps (≤ 0.1 Hz) near each natural frequency to resolve the peak accurately.
  • Damping specified: Constant damping ratio, Rayleigh damping, or material damping — must be specified or response at resonance will be infinite.
  • Force application location and direction correct.
  • Phase angle interpretation understood: 0° = in phase with force; 180° = out of phase (above resonance).
  • Peak amplitude cross-checked: Should be ≈ F₀/(k·2ζ) for SDOF approximation near resonance.
  • Solver method appropriate: Full method for accurate results; MSUP for large models (requires prior modal solve).

Transient Structural Checklist

  • Time step small enough: Δt ≤ T_min/20 where T_min = 1/f_max. ANSYS default is conservative — verify for your frequency content.
  • Total simulation time covers the event of interest including settling time for transient effects to decay.
  • Load history correctly defined: Step change, ramp, impulse, or periodic — verify the load-time curve in Tabular Data.
  • Initial conditions defined if structure starts from a non-zero state.
  • Integration method appropriate: Newmark (ANSYS default) for general dynamics; HHT-α for stiff problems.
  • Output frequency set: Store every Nth step — storing every step for long transients generates unmanageable file sizes.
  • Full transient vs MSUP: Mode Superposition is faster but cannot handle contact nonlinearity, large deflection, or plasticity.
