CFD Fundamentals

What CFD solves, how solvers work, and what you must understand before touching the mesh.

What CFD is — and is not

CFD discretises a fluid domain into cells (the mesh), then iteratively solves conservation equations at each cell until the imbalance (residual) falls below a threshold. The output is a field of pressure, velocity, temperature, and other quantities at every point in the domain.

Key principle: CFD replaces the continuous fluid domain with a finite set of algebraic equations — one per cell per transported quantity. The accuracy of the solution depends on mesh quality, boundary conditions, turbulence model choice, and convergence behaviour.
Critical: A converged residual does not mean a correct answer. Always validate results against theory, a mesh independence study, or experimental data.
