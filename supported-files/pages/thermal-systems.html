Thermal Systems & Heat Exchangers

Overall heat transfer coefficient, LMTD method, NTU-effectiveness method, and combined mode analysis.

Overall heat transfer coefficient

\[ \frac{1}{UA}=\frac{1}{h_1A_1}+\frac{\ln(r_2/r_1)}{2\pi kL}+\frac{1}{h_2A_2}\quad\text{(cylindrical)} \]
Overall U-value: \(U\) [W/m²·K]. Combines convection on both sides with wall conduction in series. For a flat wall: \(1/(UA)=1/(h_1A)+L/(kA)+1/(h_2A)\). Used in heat exchanger design: \(\dot{Q}=UA\Delta T_{lm}\).
\[ \frac{1}{U}=\frac{1}{h_i}+\frac{t_{wall}}{k_{wall}}+R_{fouling}+\frac{1}{h_o} \]
U-value with fouling: \(R_{fouling}\) = fouling resistance [m²·K/W]. TEMA standards give fouling factors for different fluids. Typical: cooling water \(\approx 2\times10^{-4}\) m²·K/W, seawater \(\approx 1\times10^{-4}\) m²·K/W.

LMTD method

\[ \dot{Q}=UA\Delta T_{lm},\quad \Delta T_{lm}=\frac{\Delta T_1-\Delta T_2}{\ln(\Delta T_1/\Delta T_2)} \]
Log Mean Temperature Difference: \(\Delta T_1\) and \(\Delta T_2\) = temperature differences between hot and cold streams at each end of the HX. For counterflow: \(\Delta T_1 = T_{h,in}-T_{c,out}\), \(\Delta T_2 = T_{h,out}-T_{c,in}\). Apply correction factor \(F\) for non-counterflow configurations: \(\dot{Q}=UAF\Delta T_{lm,cf}\).
Use LMTD when: All four terminal temperatures are known. Use NTU-ε when: Outlet temperatures are unknown — you know the HX geometry (UA) and want to find performance.

NTU-effectiveness method

\[ \varepsilon=\frac{\dot{Q}}{\dot{Q}_{max}},\quad \dot{Q}_{max}=C_{min}(T_{h,in}-T_{c,in}) \]
Effectiveness: \(\varepsilon\) = [0–1]. \(C_{min}=(\dot{m}c_p)_{min}\) = smaller heat capacity rate of the two streams [W/K]. Maximum possible heat transfer if the HX were infinitely long.
\[ NTU=\frac{UA}{C_{min}},\quad C_r=\frac{C_{min}}{C_{max}} \]
Number of Transfer Units: NTU is the dimensionless HX size. \(C_r\) = capacity ratio [0–1]. For a counterflow HX: \(\varepsilon = [1-\exp(-NTU(1-C_r))]\,/\,[1-C_r\exp(-NTU(1-C_r))]\). For a condenser/evaporator (\(C_r=0\)): \(\varepsilon=1-\exp(-NTU)\).

Energy balance on fluid streams

\[ \dot{Q}=\dot{m}c_p(T_{out}-T_{in})\quad\text{(per stream)} \]
Stream energy balance: Hot side loses heat, cold side gains heat (plus losses to surroundings, if any). This equation applied to both streams gives the two-equation system needed to find all temperatures given \(\dot{Q}\). In CFD validation: check that this is satisfied at the inlet and outlet planes using surface integrals.
\[ \dot{m}_h c_{p,h}(T_{h,in}-T_{h,out})=\dot{m}_c c_{p,c}(T_{c,out}-T_{c,in})\quad\text{(no heat loss to surroundings)} \]
Heat balance across the HX: Used to verify both LMTD and NTU calculations. Any imbalance indicates either measurement error, heat loss to surroundings, or phase change.
