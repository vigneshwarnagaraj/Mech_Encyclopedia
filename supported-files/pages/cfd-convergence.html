Convergence & Mesh Independence

How to know when your simulation is actually converged, and how to verify your mesh is adequate.

Residual convergence

Residuals measure the imbalance in the governing equations across all cells. A drop of 3–4 orders of magnitude is often cited as convergence, but this is a necessary condition, not a sufficient one.

Residual behaviourWhat it meansAction
Steady decline to 10⁻⁴ or lowerGood convergence progressCheck that physical monitors have also stabilised
Levelling off at 10⁻³May indicate a physically unsteady regionConsider transient solver; check if physics monitor is still changing
Oscillation that does not reduceUnsteady physics, or numerical instabilityCheck BCs, mesh quality, reduce relaxation factors
Divergence / blow-upNumerical instability — solver cannot stabiliseReduce relaxation, check mesh, check BC signs
Never judge convergence only by residuals. Monitor a physical output — pressure drop, lift, drag, outlet temperature. If it has stopped changing, you are converged in a useful engineering sense.

Setting up physical monitors

  • Pressure drop: Difference between inlet and outlet area-weighted average pressure. Should stabilise to a constant value.
  • Drag force: Reports → Forces → choose wall zones → plot total force vs. iteration.
  • Outlet temperature: Surface integrals → area-weighted average temperature at outlet plane.
  • Lift coefficient: For external aerodynamics — monitors the primary output of the simulation.
  • Mass flow imbalance: (Mass flow in − mass flow out)/mass flow in × 100%. Target: < 0.1%.

Mesh independence study

\[ GCI_{fine}=F_s\left|\frac{\phi_1-\phi_2}{\phi_2}\right|\frac{1}{r^p-1} \]
Grid Convergence Index (Richardson extrapolation): \(\phi_1\) = fine mesh result, \(\phi_2\) = coarse mesh result, \(r\) = mesh refinement ratio (\(\geq1.3\)), \(p\) = formal order of accuracy (typically 2), \(F_s\) = safety factor (1.25 for 3 meshes). GCI < 5% is typically acceptable. Quantifies discretisation error.
Practical procedure: Run coarse, medium, fine mesh (each ≈3× the previous in cell count). Compare a key scalar output (pressure drop, drag, \(T_{outlet}\)). If medium → fine changes < 2–5%, medium mesh is adequate. Report the GCI or the % change.
