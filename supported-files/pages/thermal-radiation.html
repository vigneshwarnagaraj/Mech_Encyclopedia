Thermal Radiation

Blackbody emission, Stefan-Boltzmann law, emissivity, view factors, and radiation between surfaces.

Blackbody radiation

\[ E_b=\sigma T^4,\quad \sigma=5.67\times10^{-8}\;\text{W/m}^2\text{·K}^4 \]
Stefan-Boltzmann law: \(E_b\) = blackbody emissive power [W/m²]. Maximum possible emission from any surface at temperature \(T\) [K]. Must use absolute temperature (Kelvin).
W/m²T in Kelvin — never Celsius
\[ \lambda_{max}T=2898\;\mu\text{m·K}\quad\text{(Wien's displacement law)} \]
Wien's displacement law: Wavelength of peak emission × temperature = constant. At room temperature (300 K): \(\lambda_{max}\approx9.7\;\mu\)m (infrared). At the sun surface (5800 K): \(\lambda_{max}\approx0.5\;\mu\)m (visible yellow-green).

Real surfaces — emissivity

\[ \dot{Q}_{rad}=\varepsilon\sigma A(T_s^4-T_{sur}^4) \]
Radiation heat transfer from a real surface: \(\varepsilon\) = emissivity [0–1], \(T_s\) = surface temperature [K], \(T_{sur}\) = surroundings temperature [K], \(A\) = surface area [m²].
Typical emissivity values: polished aluminium \(\approx0.05\), polished copper \(\approx0.03\), oxidised steel \(\approx0.7\), black paint \(\approx0.95\), human skin \(\approx0.95\).
Important: Radiation heat transfer depends on \(T^4\) — not on \(\Delta T\). At low temperatures (<200 °C), radiation is usually negligible compared to convection. At furnace/combustion temperatures (>800 °C), radiation dominates.

View factors

\[ F_{12}=\frac{1}{A_1}\int_{A_1}\int_{A_2}\frac{\cos\theta_1\cos\theta_2}{\pi r^2}\,dA_2\,dA_1 \]
View factor \(F_{ij}\): Fraction of radiation leaving surface \(i\) that reaches surface \(j\) directly. Key properties:
  • Reciprocity: \(A_i F_{ij} = A_j F_{ji}\)
  • Summation: \(\sum_{j=1}^N F_{ij}=1\) (all radiation must go somewhere)
  • Flat or convex surface: \(F_{ii}=0\) (cannot see itself)
\[ \dot{Q}_{1\to2}=\frac{\sigma(T_1^4-T_2^4)}{\dfrac{1-\varepsilon_1}{\varepsilon_1 A_1}+\dfrac{1}{A_1 F_{12}}+\dfrac{1-\varepsilon_2}{\varepsilon_2 A_2}} \]
Radiation exchange between two gray surfaces: Each term in the denominator is a resistance: surface 1 emission resistance + space resistance + surface 2 emission resistance. For two large parallel plates: \(\dot{Q}/A=\sigma(T_1^4-T_2^4)/(1/\varepsilon_1+1/\varepsilon_2-1)\).

When to enable radiation in CFD: If surface temperatures exceed ~400–500 °C or if you are simulating solar loading, furnace environments, building thermal comfort, or any situation where emissive power is comparable to convective flux.
