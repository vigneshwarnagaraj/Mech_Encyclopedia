Model & Formula Selection Guides

How to choose the right turbulence model, structural analysis type, heat transfer model, and vibration approach for any engineering problem.

CFD turbulence model selection

ScenarioRecommended modelAvoid
Fully enclosed duct, no separation, high Rek-ε Realizable or StandardLES (unnecessary cost)
External aerodynamics — car, aircraft wing, buildingSST k-ωStandard k-ε (misses separation)
Turbomachinery, fans, pumpsRNG k-ε or SST k-ωStandard k-ε near walls
Bluff body with large separationSST k-ω or Scale-Adaptive Simulation (SAS)k-ε (over-predicts attachment)
Acoustic noise, combustion, unsteady wakeLES or DESAny RANS (time-averages out structure)
Low Reynolds number (Re < 2000)LaminarAny turbulence model (unphysical)
Natural convection + turbulenceLow-Re k-ε or SST with buoyancy terms enabledHigh-Re wall functions
Transitional flow (near critical Re)Gamma-ReTheta (γ-Reθ) transition modelFully turbulent RANS
Impinging jet heat transferk-ε RealizableStandard k-ε (over-predicts stagnation heat transfer)
Quick rule: When unsure between k-ε and SST, choose SST. It performs better across more scenarios with negligible extra cost for most industrial problems.

Heat transfer model selection

ScenarioPrimary mechanismModel / formula
Solid wall, known temperatures both sidesConductionFourier's law; R_cond = L/(kA)
Surface exposed to moving fluidForced convectionNewton's cooling + Nusselt correlation
Heated vertical surface in still airNatural convectionRa → Nu correlation for vertical plate
High temperature surface (T > 500 °C)Radiation significantStefan-Boltzmann + emissivity
Furnace / combustion environmentRadiation dominantView factors + DO model in CFD
Small object cooling quickly, Bi < 0.1Convection + transientLumped capacitance: T(t) = T∞ + (T₀−T∞)e^(−t/τ)
Two fluid streams exchanging heatCombinedLMTD (known temperatures) or NTU-ε (unknown outlet)
Heat sink fin array on PCBConduction in fin + convection off surfaceFin efficiency η + extended surface formula
