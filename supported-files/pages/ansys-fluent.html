Fluent Meshing — controls and what they do

ControlWhat it doesWhen to use
Global element sizeSets maximum cell size across the entire domainFirst pass; override locally for refinement

Edge sizingControls node count on selected edgesLeading edge, nozzle throat, sharp geometric features
Inflation layersAdds thin prism cells normal to wall surfacesWhenever wall shear, y+, heat transfer, or boundary layer matters

Inflation layer setup

\[ \Delta y_1=\frac{y^+\,\mu}{\rho\,u_\tau},\quad u_\tau=\sqrt{\frac{\tau_w}{\rho}}\approx\sqrt{\frac{C_f}{2}}\cdot V_\infty \]
First layer height calculation: Set target \(y^+\) (1 for wall-resolved SST, 30–300 for k-ε wall functions). Estimate \(C_f\) from flat plate: \(C_f \approx 0.026\,Re_L^{-1/7}\) (turbulent). Compute \(u_\tau\), then \(\Delta y_1\). Number of layers: 5–20. Growth rate: 1.2.
Quick estimate for air at 30 m/s over 0.5 m flat plate targeting y⁺=1: Re = 1.03×10⁶ → C_f ≈ 0.00356 → τ_w = 2.39 Pa → u_τ = 1.40 m/s → Δy₁ = 1.04×10⁻⁵ m ≈ 10 μm. Use an online y+ calculator to check this for your specific geometry.

Solver settings and convergence

SettingDefault valueGuidance

k and ε relaxation0.8Reduce to 0.5–0.6 if turbulence oscillates
Energy relaxation1.0Reduce to 0.9 if temperature diverges
Pressure interpolationStandard (SIMPLE) / Second OrderUse Second Order for accuracy in final run
Momentum schemeSecond Order UpwindStart with First Order for convergence, switch to Second Order for final result
Iterations (steady)—Min 500; complex flows 1000–3000; stop when monitors flatten
Time step (transient)—Target CFL ≤ 1 in regions of interest; LES: CFL ≈ 0.5–1 near walls
Convergence criteria10⁻³ for continuity/velocity, 10⁻⁶ for energyTighten energy to 10⁻⁷ for precise heat transfer; never relax continuity above 10⁻³
Critical workflow: Always monitor a physical quantity (pressure drop, drag, outlet temperature) in addition to residuals. Never stop a simulation solely because residuals hit a threshold — check that the monitor is also flat.
