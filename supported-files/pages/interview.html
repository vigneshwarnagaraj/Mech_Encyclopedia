Model selection questions — strong answers

QuestionStrong answer
"Which turbulence model did you use and why?""SST k-ω because the flow has adverse pressure gradients and possible separation near the trailing edge. It uses k-ω near walls — accurate in the sublayer — and switches to k-ε in the freestream for stability. For a fully enclosed turbulent duct I would have used Realizable k-ε instead."
"Why did you use static structural and not transient?""The load varies slowly relative to the system's natural frequency — the dynamic amplification factor is approximately 1. Inertia effects are negligible, so static analysis is appropriate and conservative in the sense that it avoids the complexity of time-stepping without losing accuracy."
"Why is the energy equation turned off?""The flow is isothermal — no temperature gradients, heat transfer, or buoyancy effects. Enabling it would add transported scalar equations for temperature and turbulent heat flux without changing the velocity or pressure field."
"What is the difference between k-ε and SST k-ω?""Standard k-ε uses wall functions and performs well for fully turbulent attached flows but over-predicts eddy viscosity in adverse pressure gradients, causing it to delay separation. SST k-ω blends k-ω accuracy in the near-wall region with k-ε stability in the freestream, making it better for flows with significant separation — external aerodynamics, automotive, turbomachinery."
"When would you use lumped capacitance?""When Bi < 0.1, meaning the internal conduction resistance is much less than the external convection resistance, so the object is approximately isothermal throughout. For Bi ≥ 0.1, I would use Heisler charts or solve the full unsteady diffusion equation numerically."

Common interview traps

Trap questionWrong answerCorrect answer
"Residuals at 10⁻⁴ — is it converged?""Yes, that's converged.""Residuals dropping is necessary but not sufficient. I also check that physical monitors (drag, ΔP, T_outlet) have stabilised independently."
"FEA shows 800 MPa at a corner — is the part failing?""Yes, it exceeds yield.""That peak is likely a stress singularity at a sharp corner — a mesh artifact. I need to add a fillet radius and recheck. Singularities grow monotonically with mesh refinement."
"What does a high Biot number mean?""Good thermal resistance.""Bi > 0.1 means internal conduction resistance is significant — the body has a non-uniform temperature field. Lumped capacitance is no longer valid; I must solve the full diffusion equation."
"What does mode shape tell you?""How much the structure vibrates.""Mode shape shows the deformation pattern at a natural frequency — where nodes (zero motion) and antinodes (maximum motion) are. It tells me where to add stiffness or damping to shift or damp that mode most effectively."
"Is a small residual proof of a correct CFD result?""Yes.""No. Residuals measure the imbalance in discretised equations. A wrongly set up model with consistent but incorrect BCs can converge to a wrong solution with perfect residuals. Validation against theory or experiment is always required."
"Why did you add inflation layers?""Because everyone does it.""To resolve the steep velocity gradient normal to the wall, control y+, and obtain accurate wall shear stress and heat transfer. Without them, the near-wall physics is poorly resolved regardless of how fine the rest of the mesh is."

Plain English summaries

ConceptPlain English
Reynolds numberHow hard the flow is pushing (inertia) versus how hard the fluid resists shearing (viscosity). High Re = turbulent; low Re = laminar.
Nusselt numberHow many times more heat is removed by convection than would be by conduction alone through the same fluid layer. Nu = 1 = pure conduction; Nu = 100 = convection is 100× more effective.
Von Mises stressA single number that combines all stress components and answers: "Would this stress state cause a ductile metal to yield?" If it exceeds yield strength, plastic deformation begins.
Damping ratio ζHow quickly vibration dies out. ζ = 0: oscillates forever. ζ = 1: returns to rest as fast as possible without overshooting. ζ = 0.05: typical lightly damped metal — takes about 10 cycles to decay significantly.
FEA stiffness matrixA giant table of springs connecting all nodes. Each entry says "if you push node X in direction Y, how much does node Z move?" Solving [K]{u} = {F} finds all displacements simultaneously.
y+ (y-plus)Dimensionless distance from the wall. It tells the CFD solver which region of the boundary layer the first cell is in — and whether the near-wall model is being used in its valid range.
Mesh independenceProof that your answer does not depend on how finely you divided the domain. If making the mesh finer does not change your key result by more than ~2%, the answer is a property of the physics — not an artifact of the mesh.
Transmissibility TRThe ratio of vibration output to input. TR < 1 means you are in the isolation region — the mount is doing its job. TR > 1 means the mount is amplifying the vibration — you are near or below the isolation threshold.
