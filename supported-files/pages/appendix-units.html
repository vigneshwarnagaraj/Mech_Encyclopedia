Temperature Conversions

\[ T[\text{K}]=T[°\text{C}]+273.15,\quad T[°\text{C}]=T[\text{K}]-273.15 \]
Use K in: radiation (T⁴), ideal gas law, Carnot efficiency, thermal diffusivity equations. Use °C or K interchangeably for temperature differences ΔT.
\[ T[°\text{C}]=\tfrac{5}{9}(T[°\text{F}]-32),\quad T[°\text{F}]=\tfrac{9}{5}T[°\text{C}]+32 \]
Fahrenheit used in US engineering, HVAC, and legacy standards. Convert to °C or K before any formula application.
