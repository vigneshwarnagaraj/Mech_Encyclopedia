Theory of Machines · 15
Mechanisms & Rotation

Angular kinematics, gear drives, belt and chain drives, and cam/follower basics.

Angular kinematics

\[ \omega=\frac{d\theta}{dt},\quad\alpha=\frac{d\omega}{dt},\quad v=r\omega,\quad a_t=r\alpha,\quad a_n=r\omega^2 \]
Symbols: \(\theta\) = angular displacement [rad], \(\omega\) = angular velocity [rad/s], \(\alpha\) = angular acceleration [rad/s²], \(r\) = radius [m], \(a_t\) = tangential acceleration [m/s²], \(a_n\) = centripetal acceleration [m/s²] (directed inward).
\[ \omega=\frac{2\pi N}{60},\quad\text{where }N=\text{rpm} \]
rpm to rad/s conversion: Needed whenever motor speed in rpm must be converted for torque-power calculations or vibration analysis. \(P=T\omega\) [W] requires \(\omega\) in rad/s.

Gear drives

\[ i=\frac{\omega_{in}}{\omega_{out}}=\frac{N_{out}}{N_{in}}=\frac{T_{out}}{T_{in}\cdot\eta} \]
Gear ratio \(i\): \(N\) = number of teeth, \(\eta\) = mechanical efficiency. Gear increases torque at the cost of speed (and vice versa). Ideal: power in = power out (\(T\omega\) = const). Real: power out = \(\eta\) × power in.
\[ m_g=\frac{d}{N}\quad[\text{mm/tooth}] \]
Module \(m_g\): Standard gear sizing parameter. \(d\) = pitch circle diameter [mm]. Two meshing gears must have the same module. Common metric modules: 1, 1.25, 1.5, 2, 2.5, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 10.
\[ v_t=\frac{\pi d n}{60\,000}\;\text{m/s},\quad P=F_t\cdot v_t \]
Pitch line velocity and transmitted power: \(d\) = pitch diameter [mm], \(n\) = speed [rpm], \(F_t\) = tangential tooth load [N], \(P\) = power [W]. The tangential load is used to size gear tooth strength (Lewis beam strength).
\[ F_t=\frac{2T}{d},\quad F_r=F_t\tan\phi,\quad F_a=0\;\text{(spur gear)} \]
Gear tooth forces (spur gear): \(F_t\) = tangential force, \(F_r\) = radial force (separating force), \(\phi\) = pressure angle (standard: 20°). Helical gears also have \(F_a\) = axial (thrust) force.

Belt and chain drives

\[ \frac{T_1}{T_2}=e^{\mu\theta} \]
Euler-Eytelwein belt friction equation: \(T_1\) = tight side tension [N], \(T_2\) = slack side tension [N], \(\mu\) = coefficient of friction between belt and pulley, \(\theta\) = angle of wrap [rad]. Slipping starts when this ratio is exceeded.
\[ P=\left(T_1-T_2\right)v,\quad T_{initial}=\frac{T_1+T_2}{2} \]
Power and initial tension: \(v\) = belt velocity [m/s], \(T_{initial}\) = tension when drive is at rest — used to size bearings. Centrifugal tension: \(T_c=mv^2\) where \(m\) = belt mass per unit length [kg/m].

Four-bar linkage kinematics

\[ \frac{\omega_3}{\omega_2}=\frac{\vec{r}_2\times\vec{r}_{32}}{\vec{r}_3\times\vec{r}_{32}}\quad\text{(velocity ratio)} \]
Four-bar mechanism: Links: crank (2), coupler (3), rocker (4), ground (1). Velocity and acceleration analysis uses loop-closure equations. Types: crank-rocker (Grashof condition: shortest link + longest link ≤ sum of other two).
